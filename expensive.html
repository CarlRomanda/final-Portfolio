Top 10 Expensive cars

All of these high-end luxury cars on this top 10 list are high performance super cars. Some are sensations in the looks department, while others are quite an acquired taste to say the least.

10

Aston Martin Valkyrie -US$3.2 Million

An incredibly special car with an equally remarkable name. Valkyrie is Aston Martin's first ever hypercar and it leaves nothing in reserve.
It has a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine, that produces 1,130 horsepower.
It has a light-weight carbon fiber chassis and was developed as a track oriented car.

9

Limited Edition Bugatti Veyron by Mansory Vivere - US$3.4 Million

The Mansory Vivere edition was styled after the Grand Sports Vitesse Roadster, and it features a customized cabin, a redesigned spoiler and front grille, as well as upgraded LED lights
It has a 8.0-liter W16 engine that produces 1,200 horsepower and it was styled with a custom carbon-fiber body by Mansory, a German company that specializes in customizing luxury vehicles..
The Bugatti Veyron is one of the most seminal supercars of the 21st century. At the time, it was the most powerful production car in the world when it launched in 2005, and, despite being surpassed by the 1,500-hp Chiron, is still sought-after by car collectors.

8

Lykan Hypersport US$3.4 Million

With only 7 units in existence, the Lykan HyperSport is one of the most exclusive cars on the planet. If that is not enough, the Lykan features the world’s first holographic mid-air display with interactive motion control and it has 440 diamonds lined in the LED headlights.
It featured in the blockbuster film Furious 7 and it has a 3.7-liter twin-turbocharged flat-six engine, that produces 780 horsepower.
With a hand-crafted carbon fiber body, the Lykan Hypersport has a timeless look and angular styling. Its patented reverse dihedral door system accentuates its lines, and the dynamic spoiler completes the exclusive design.

7

McLaren P1 LM - US$3.6 Million

The P1 LM is 60kg lighter than the P1 GTR thanks to a number of weight-saving measures. The exhaust headers and catalytic convertor pipes are made from Inconel, saving 4.5kg, and race parts like the air jack system have been removed and lightweight seats from the F1 GTR are used.
Only 6 of these luxury sports cars exist and they all have a large twin-turbocharged V8 engine, that produces over 1,000 horsepower.
The P1 LM is claimed to be the quickest, rarest and last of the P1 variants. Just five production models are being made.

6

Lamborghini Veneno - US$4.5 Million

The Lamborghini Veneno looks like a venomous snake and that’s exactly what its name means Veneno means “venom” in Italian.
It’s based on the Aventador roadster and was made to celebrate Lamborghini’s 50th anniversary.
The Veneno is powered by a 6.5-liter V12 engine, that produces 740 horsepower, and it has a top speed of 221 mph.

5

Koenigsegg CCXR Trevita – US$4.8 Million

It features a unique double carbon rear wing, inconell exhaust system, carbon ceramic ABS brakes and a tire monitoring system.
The Trevita was originally intended to be a limited edition of three examples. The white carbon fibre used for the Trevita made the car extremely difficult and time consuming to manufacture, however, and a decision was taken to make the car even more exclusive and limit the number of Trevitas made to just two.
It’s powered by a 4.7-liter twin-supercharged V8, that produces 1,004 horsepower.

4

Bugatti Divo – US$5.8 Million

The Bugatti Divo is a mid-engine track-focused sports car developed and manufactured by Bugatti Automobiles S.A.S. The car is named after French racing driver Albert Divo, who raced for Bugatti in the 1920s winning the Targa Florio race twice.
Only 40 units were ever made and they all have a ‎8.0-liter quad-turbocharged W16 engine, that produces 1,479 horsepower!
The Divo is BUGATTI’s first coach-built hyper sports car of the 21st century. The limited and extremely high-performance hyper sports car is an absolute must-have for any BUGATTI enthusiast’s hyper sports car collection.

3

Mercedes Benz Maybach Exelero – US$8.0 Million

This high performance 4 seater comes equipped with a twin-turbo V12 engine, that produces 690 horsepower.
The Exelero is powered by a twin turbo V12 engine made by the defunct Maybach (now a part of the Mercedes-Benz division of Daimler AG), and mounted at 60º.
Mercedes built the Exelero on the bones of a Maybach, and gave it the same twin-turbo V12 engine producing 690 horsepower (510 kilowatts) and 752 pound-feet (1,020 Newton-meters) of torque.

2

Rolls-Royce Sweptail – US$13 Million

the Rolls Royce Sweptail – a bespoke one-off built for one of Rolls’ “most valued customers”. Inspired by the “swept-tail” Rolls Royces of the Twenties and Thirties, work began on the Sweptail in 2013, when an unnamed, exceptionally wealthy fellow asked Rolls to build him a “one-off luxury motor car like no other”.
It was commissioned in 2013, by a luxury yacht and aircraft designer whose identity still remains unknown.
The hand-built Sweptail is based on the Phantom platform and it has a 6.75-liter V12 engine, that produces 453 horsepower.

1

Bugatti La Voiture Noire – US$19 Million

Bugatti set off an avalanche of buzz when it revealed its La Voiture Noire at the 2019 Geneva Motor Show. Not long after, news outlets reported that the Noire had been purchased by an unnamed buyer for the highest new-car price ever — $18.7 million. So far, the identity of the buyer remains a mystery.
La Voiture Noire means “the black car” in French and the identity of its owner is unknown…
It’s based on the Chiron model and it has the same engine as the Chiron, a quad-turbocharged 8.0-liter W16, that produces 1,500 horsepower!
